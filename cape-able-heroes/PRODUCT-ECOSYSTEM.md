# Cape-Able Heroes – Product Ecosystem Map (v1)

**Purpose:** Living map of the long-term Cape-Able Heroes product vision — anchored in field evidence and governed by the SAGA loop. Not a wish list. Not a pitch deck. A structured record of what is proven, what is hypothesized, and what the evidence-based path to each product looks like.  
**Status:** v1 — active for Phase 4. Update as evidence accumulates.

---

## Governing Principle

**Every product on this map must earn its place with field evidence.**

A product is added to the "Ready to Build" column only when:
1. Real customers have asked for it OR field data shows a clear gap it would close
2. A minimum viable version has been defined
3. A SAGA proposal has been filed for it

Products in the "Hypothesized" column are ideas that seem promising — but have not yet been validated.

This map is not aspiration. It is the difference between building what the market needs and building what we imagine the market wants.

---

## The Core Offer (Anchor Point)

Everything in the product ecosystem connects back to this:

> **The WonderLab experience** — a governed, identity-first, evidence-based session that moves a child from hesitation to participation.

All products either:
- **Extend the WonderLab** (before or after the session)
- **Replicate the WonderLab** (so it can happen without Rian)
- **Scale the WonderLab** (so more children can access the core transformation)

If a product does none of these three things, it does not belong in this ecosystem.

### Layer 3 — Hypothesized (Promising but Not Yet Proven)

These have appeared in brainstorms, customer conversations, or market observations — but have not been validated with field evidence. Do not build any of these without a SAGA proposal backed by real demand signals.

| Product | What It Is | Hypothesis | Validation Needed |
|---------|-----------|-----------|------------------|
| Cape-Able Heroes Storybook Series | Illustrated books featuring children using a Cape-Able Strength to work through a real challenge | Parents will purchase books that reinforce the identity model at home | Parent surveys + book prototype test at 3+ sessions |
| At-Home Challenge Kit (subscription or one-off) | Monthly or seasonal kit with themed challenges for families | Parents want continuation after the WonderLab; a structured kit reduces the "what do we do now?" drop-off | Test demand with post-session offers to real parents |
| Digital Mission Cards | App or web-based Hero Cards and challenge prompts | Some parents prefer digital artifacts | Survey current WonderLab families; demand must exceed 30% expressed interest before building |
| Cape-Able Heroes Audio Experience | Audio stories where children follow a hero through a challenge, with built-in pause points for real participation | Could extend the experience to contexts without a facilitator | Pilot with one audio story and measure engagement |
| Parent Coaching Add-On | Short coaching call or guide for parents who want to continue the identity work at home | Parents who experience the WonderLab will want to know how to continue it | Offer this post-session and measure uptake |
| School Curriculum Integration | Multi-session SEL curriculum built on the WonderLab framework | Schools want repeatable, standards-aligned programming | Requires Tier 2 licensing to be active + 2+ school partners |
| Cape-Able Heroes App | A digital platform combining hero identity, challenge tracking, and parent visibility | Consolidates several digital components into one product | Do not build until Layer 3 digital components have been individually validated |

## How to Update This Map

- After every SAGA Analyze cycle, review the Evidence-to-Product Pathway table and update "Current Signal."
- When a product earns enough evidence, move it from Hypothesized → Layer 2, and file a SAGA proposal.
- When a product fails validation (customers clearly do not want it), move it to the No-Build log below.
- Never move a product forward based on enthusiasm alone.
