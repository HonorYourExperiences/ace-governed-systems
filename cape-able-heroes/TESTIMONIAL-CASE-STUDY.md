# Cape-Able Heroes – Testimonial & Case Study Templates (v1)

**Purpose:** Capture real outcomes and parent/child/educator feedback in a format usable for marketing, grant applications, school partnerships, and future product development.  
**Status:** v1 — active for Phase 3.

---

## Why This Matters

A testimonial is not marketing copy. It is evidence.

The difference: marketing copy says what we want people to believe. A testimonial records what someone actually observed, in their own words. The governing axiom here is **Evidence & Capability** — we do not manufacture social proof, we document real outcomes.

A case study is a complete story: what happened, what changed, and what it means.

---

## Part 1 — Testimonial Collection

### When to Collect

- Within 24 hours of a paid session (warm, experience is fresh)
- 1 week after, via follow-up (more considered, reflects at-home impact)
- When a parent, educator, or child says something specific and meaningful — capture it immediately

### How to Ask (Follow-Up Message — use this from the PAID-ACTIVATION-PLAYBOOK)

> Could you answer one or two of these questions?
> 1. What did you notice in your child (or the children) during or after the session?
> 2. Is there anything you'd want another parent (or principal, or camp director) to know before booking?

## Part 3 — Testimonial Usage Guide

### Where Testimonials Can Be Used

| Use Case | What to include |
|----------|----------------|
| School/camp outreach | 1–2 quotes from educators or program leaders |
| Parent outreach | 1–2 quotes from parents — focus on observable child behavior |
| Website / booking page | The strongest 2–3 lines (with attribution and permission) |
| Grant or partnership applications | Full case study + specific outcome data |
| Social media | Short direct quotes — always attributed correctly |

### Language Guidelines

- Use exact words. Do not smooth out grammar unless the person asks you to.
- Never attribute more certainty than the person expressed. ("My daughter seemed more confident" is not the same as "My daughter is more confident.")
- Always note the attribution level the person agreed to (full name, first name only, anonymous).
- Never manufacture, combine, or paraphrase. If you do not have a real quote, do not create one.
