# Cape-Able Heroes – Licensing & Partner Program (v1)

**Purpose:** Govern how external individuals and organizations can deliver Cape-Able Heroes WonderLabs under an official license — maintaining quality, identity, and axiomatic fidelity without requiring the founder in every room.  
**Status:** v1 — structure and standards ready. Activate when first partner candidate is identified.

---

## Why a Licensing Program

The WonderLab experience is not just content — it is a governed *way of being with children*.

A licensing program is only worth building if it preserves what makes Cape-Able Heroes real: the witnessing model, the identity-first design, the refusal to rescue, and the commitment to evidence over aspiration. A license that produces lower-quality versions of the experience is not a business asset — it is a liability and a violation of the governing axioms.

The program is therefore designed with quality as the moat, not volume.

---

## Licensing Tiers

### Tier 1 — Certified Facilitator

**Who it's for:** Individual educators, coaches, youth workers, counselors, and program leaders who want to run WonderLabs independently in their own context.

**What they get:**
- Full Facilitator Certification (training + assessment — see Certification section below)
- Licensed use of all Cape-Able Heroes session materials (SESSION-FORMAT.md, CHALLENGE-CARDS.md, Observation Tool, Feedback Forms)
- Access to future material updates
- Listing on Cape-Able Heroes facilitator directory (when live)
- Annual renewal and re-certification pathway

**What they pay:**
- Initial certification fee: $297–$497 (to be confirmed after first cohort)
- Annual renewal: $97–$197

**What they cannot do:**
- Sub-license to others
- Alter the core session structure or governing language
- Use Cape-Able Heroes branding for unrelated offerings
- Represent themselves as BYDT employees or brand representatives

**Revenue to BYDT:** Certification fee + annual renewal. No per-session royalty at this tier.

---

### Tier 2 — Institutional License

**Who it's for:** Schools, camps, after-school programs, community organizations that want to train multiple staff members and run Cape-Able Heroes as an ongoing part of their programming.

**What they get:**
- Organization-wide facilitator certification (up to [N] staff members per year)
- Licensed use of all materials + institutional branding kit (co-branded)
- Priority support and access to new themes and materials
- Annual impact review with BYDT (SAGA-governed)

**What they pay:**
- Annual institutional license: $997–$2,997 (based on organization size)
- Per-staff certification if exceeding the included headcount

**Revenue to BYDT:** Annual license fee + per-staff overage. No per-session royalty.

---

### Tier 3 — Regional Partner

**Who it's for:** Organizations or individuals who want to represent Cape-Able Heroes in a specific geography — running their own facilitator training cohorts and building local programming infrastructure.

**What they get:**
- Exclusive or preferred territory rights (defined region, 12-month terms)
- Authorization to train Tier 1 facilitators under BYDT certification standards
- Full materials library + co-branded regional assets
- Revenue share on Tier 1 certifications issued in their territory

**What they pay:**
- Regional partner fee: TBD based on territory size and proposed activation plan
- Must meet minimum activation targets (sessions run, facilitators certified) to maintain territory exclusivity

**Revenue to BYDT:** Partner fee + percentage of Tier 1 certifications issued. Revenue share model to be defined before first partner.

**Note:** This tier should not be launched until at least 5 Tier 1 facilitators have been certified and the quality standard is proven replicable.

## Certification Program

### Purpose

Certification ensures that any licensed facilitator has internalized the governing principles of Cape-Able Heroes — not just the script, but the *why* behind every design decision.

A certified facilitator understands:
- Why identity comes before challenge (not after)
- Why the badge is given at the start, not earned at the end
- What "witnessing" means and what it requires of a facilitator
- The difference between rescue and support — and how to hold the line
- How to use the Observation Tool to capture evidence, not performance

### Certification Path

**Step 1 — Study (Self-paced)**
Read and internalize:
- SESSION-FORMAT.md (complete, with facilitator notes)
- CHALLENGE-CARDS.md (all themes)
- OBSERVATION-TOOL.md
- FEEDBACK-FORM.md (both forms)
- The five core axioms (from IMMUTABLE_VS_MUTABLE_LAYERS.md)

**Step 2 — Training Session (Live, with Rian or authorized Tier 3 partner)**
- 2–3 hour live session (in-person or video)
- Walk through a full WonderLab session as a participant, then as a facilitator
- Debrief against the governing principles

**Step 3 — Observation Run**
- Co-facilitate one full WonderLab session with a certified facilitator observing
- Receive written debrief and assessment

**Step 4 — Solo Run with Review**
- Deliver one WonderLab session independently
- Submit Observation Tool and Facilitator Debrief to BYDT for review
- Certification issued on passing review

**Assessment Standard:** Pass/no-pass. The question is not "Did this go well?" It is: "Did this facilitator maintain the governing principles under real conditions?"

## Quality & Governance Standards

All licensed facilitators and partners are bound to the following:

1. **Identity before challenge.** The Hero Identity Moment always precedes the Challenge Activity. This sequence is non-negotiable.

2. **No rescue.** Facilitators do not solve problems for children. They create conditions and ask questions. Any systematic deviation from this is grounds for license review.

3. **Evidence, not aspiration.** Facilitators do not promise outcomes. They document what actually happened. Observation Tool sheets must be completed honestly.

4. **Witnessing language.** Facilitators name what they see, not what they feel. ("I see you kept trying" — not "I'm so proud of you.")

5. **Reporting.** Tier 1 and Tier 2 licensees submit a brief session summary (session count, child count, notable observations) to BYDT annually for renewal. Tier 3 partners submit quarterly.

## Revocation Policy

A license may be reviewed or revoked if a licensee:
- Delivers the WonderLab in a way that materially violates the governing principles (rescue, conditional worth framing, aspiration theater)
- Uses Cape-Able Heroes branding for non-WonderLab offerings without authorization
- Fails to submit required reporting for renewal
- Misrepresents outcomes, testimonials, or evidence

Revocation is a governance decision — not a punitive one. It is logged as a SAGA event and reviewed before any action is taken.
