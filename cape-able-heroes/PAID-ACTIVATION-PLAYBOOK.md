# Cape-Able Heroes – Paid Activation Playbook (v1)

**Purpose:** Step-by-step guide for delivering a paid WonderLab activation — from booking through post-session follow-up.  
**Pricing Reference:** $250–$750 (small groups, community) · $750–$2,500+ (schools, camps, institutions)  
**Status:** v1 — refine after each activation.

---

## What a Paid Activation Is

A paid WonderLab activation is a booked, revenue-generating session where an individual, family group, school, camp, or community organization has agreed to pay for the Cape-Able Heroes experience.

Every paid activation is also a **data event** — evidence that goes into the CHANNEL-TESTING-TRACKER and SESSION-LOG.

---

## Phase 1 — Booking & Confirmation

### When a buyer says yes:

1. **Confirm the key details in writing** (email, message — anything documented):
   - Date and time
   - Location (their space or yours)
   - Number of children and age range
   - Theme preference (aviation, nature, engineering, storytelling, mapping — or facilitator's choice)
   - Agreed price and payment method

2. **Send a simple booking confirmation:**

   > Hi [Name],
   >
   > I'm confirmed for [DATE] at [TIME] at [LOCATION]. We'll be running a Cape-Able Heroes WonderLab for approximately [#] children, ages [range], with a [theme or "to-be-decided"] theme.
   >
   > Total investment: **$[AMOUNT]**. I'll [collect payment before the session / invoice you after].
   >
   > Here's what to tell the children in advance: "We're going to a WonderLab where you'll build a hero identity and take on a real challenge." That's all they need to know. No prep required.
   >
   > I'll send you a brief info sheet before we meet. Looking forward to it.
   >
   > [Your name]

3. **Log the booking in CHANNEL-TESTING-TRACKER.md** (Channel, Buyer Type, Date Booked, Revenue)

4. **Log the upcoming session in SESSION-LOG.md** (with a placeholder entry)

## Phase 2 — Preparation (48–72 Hours Before)

### Materials Checklist

- [ ] Hero Identity Cards (one per child — blank or pre-printed with name field)
- [ ] Strength list printed or displayed
- [ ] Hero Badges or Cape Elements (one per child — pre-counted)
- [ ] Challenge Cards for selected theme (one per child)
- [ ] Hero Cards for reflection phase (one per child)
- [ ] Parent Reflection Cards (one per family)
- [ ] Observation Tool sheets (one per facilitator)
- [ ] Feedback Forms — Parent version (one per family) + Facilitator debrief
- [ ] Challenge materials (theme-specific — see list below)
- [ ] Pens, markers, writing tools
- [ ] Optional: one prop or image to anchor the theme world

### Theme Materials Quick Reference

| Theme | Core Materials |
|-------|---------------|
| Aviation | Paper (multiple types), rulers, measuring tape, small targets for distance testing |
| Nature | Sticks, leaves, stones, modeling clay, small craft supplies, "creature cards" |
| Engineering/Building | Popsicle sticks, tape, paper cups, rubber bands, small weight (eraser, coin) |
| Storytelling | Blank paper panels, pencils/markers, story prompt cards |
| Mapping | Blank paper, colored pencils, "landmark" sticker set or stickers |

### Logistics Check

- [ ] Know the space — how many tables, chairs, floor space?
- [ ] Confirm arrival time (always 20–30 minutes before children arrive)
- [ ] Confirm who is managing child arrivals and parent drop-off
- [ ] Confirm if parents will be present or drop-off only
- [ ] Know how payment is being handled (before, during, or after)

## Phase 3 — Day-Of Execution

### On arrival (20–30 min early):
- Set up the room: materials at tables, cards face-down, badge/cape elements visible
- Brief any co-facilitator or assistant
- Create a "distinct space" feeling — the WonderLab should feel different from a regular room

### Run the session using SESSION-FORMAT.md:
1. Phase 1 — Arrival & World-Building (0–10 min)
2. Phase 2 — Hero Identity Moment (10–25 min)
3. Phase 3 — Challenge Activity (25–55 min)
4. Phase 4 — Reflection & Recognition (55–70 min)
5. Phase 5 — Close & Parent Handoff (70–75 min)

### During the session — capture evidence:
- Use the Observation Tool throughout Phase 3 and 4
- Note at least 3 hesitation → engagement moments (exact words or actions)
- If parents are present, briefly note their visible reactions

### At close:
- Hand out Parent Reflection Cards
- Distribute Feedback Forms — ask parents to fill them in before they leave
- Collect all completed forms before anyone exits
- If payment hasn't been collected, do so now

## Phase 4 — Post-Session (Within 24 Hours)

### Facilitator Debrief:
- Complete the Facilitator Debrief Form (FEEDBACK-FORM.md)
- Note top 3 observations and one change for next time

### Session Log:
- Complete the full SESSION-LOG.md entry for this activation

### Testimonial Collection:
- If a parent said something specific during or after the session, record the exact quote
- Send a follow-up message within 24 hours using the template below
- Log any testimonials collected in TESTIMONIAL-CASE-STUDY.md

### Channel Tracker Update:
- Update CHANNEL-TESTING-TRACKER.md with outcome (booked → ran → feedback received)

### Follow-Up Message Template:

> Hi [Name],
>
> Thank you for bringing [child's name / your students / your group] to the WonderLab today. Watching [specific thing you observed] was exactly what this is built for.
>
> I'm building a record of real outcomes from these sessions, and your perspective would be genuinely useful. If you're open to it — could you answer one or two of these questions?
>
> 1. What did you notice in your child (or the children) during or after the session?
> 2. Is there anything you'd want another parent (or principal, or camp director) to know before booking?
>
> A few sentences is plenty. I'll quote you only with your permission.
>
> Thank you again.
>
> [Your name]

## What to Do If Something Goes Wrong

**A child refuses to participate:**  
Let them watch. Do not coax, pressure, or make it conditional. Give them a role ("observer") and revisit partway through. If they never engage, that's data — note it in the Observation Tool, do not flag it as a failure.

**Materials are incomplete:**  
Simplify. Every theme has a one-material fallback: paper is always enough for any challenge. The session is the identity work, not the materials.

**Buyer disputes the price after:**  
Do not discount without cause. If there is a genuine service failure, offer a partial credit toward a future session — not a refund. If they are simply unhappy with the experience, collect their feedback honestly and decide what's warranted.

**You make a facilitation error:**  
Note it in the debrief. Do not cover it up. The SAGA loop is built on accurate field data — a session that didn't go perfectly is more useful than a session that is misreported as clean.
