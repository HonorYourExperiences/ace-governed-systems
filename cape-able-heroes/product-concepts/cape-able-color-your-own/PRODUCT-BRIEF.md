# Cape-Able Color Your Own - Product Brief

**Product family:** Cape-Able Color Your Own
**Concept type:** Physical identity artifact / at-home continuation product
**Status:** Hypothesized, validation-ready
**Primary buyer:** Parents, camps, classrooms, party/event hosts
**Primary user:** Children ages 5-12

## One-Line Offer

A set of colorable Cape-Able identity artifacts children can personalize, wear, place, or display as evidence of their own creativity and capability.

## Product Variants

### 1. Color Your Own Cape

**What it is:** A wearable coloring cape with Cape-Able line-art prompts and a hero badge/name area.

**Child action:** Color it, personalize it, wear it.

**Concept variants:**

- Washable reusable version
- Permanent keepsake version
- Mission Cape edition
- Classroom / party pack

**Best use cases:**

- WonderLab entry artifact
- Birthday or party activity
- Camp identity-building activity
- Premium take-home kit

### 2. Color Your Own Stickers

**What it is:** Printed colorable sticker sheets with Cape-Able icons, badges, phrases, and symbols.

**Child action:** Color it, peel it, place it.

**Concept variants:**

- Classic Sticker Pack
- Vinyl Keepsake Stickers
- Mission Sticker Pack
- Classroom Pack

**Best use cases:**

- Low-cost entry product
- Party favor
- Post-session continuation
- Add-on for kits and events

### 3. Color Your Own Magnets

**What it is:** Reusable colorable magnets children can display and remix on a refrigerator, whiteboard, locker, or classroom surface.

**Child action:** Color it, place it, move it.

**Concept variants:**

- Fridge Set
- Classroom Pack
- Keepsake Magnet Set
- Mission Magnets

**Best use cases:**

- Home display product
- Classroom recognition tool
- Parent-facing continuation product
- Giftable keepsake

## Why It Belongs In Cape-Able Heroes

The field evidence from the Aviation WonderLab showed that children respond strongly to tangible entry points. Not every child enters through the same kind of task. Some enter through folding, some through tools, some through decorating, some through showing others what they made.

This product line creates multiple low-barrier entry points:

- Wearable identity: the cape
- Portable expression: stickers
- Displayable/remixable identity: magnets

The shared pattern is:

> Children do not receive confidence. They create an artifact that gives them evidence of their own expression.

## Strategic Role In The Offer Ladder

This product family can serve four roles:

1. **Entry-level purchase:** Parent buys a low-cost Cape-Able product before attending a session.
2. **Post-session bridge:** Child takes home a product that continues the identity moment.
3. **Event add-on:** Host adds product packs to a WonderLab booking.
4. **Classroom/party pack:** Buyer purchases bulk kits for a group activity.

## Evidence Required Before Build

This should not move to production until at least one of the following happens:

- 5+ parents express interest in buying after a WonderLab.
- 1+ host asks for a take-home product or party/classroom pack.
- 3+ paid preorders or deposits are collected.
- A low-cost prototype test shows children use the product beyond the first coloring moment.

## Current Product Thesis

The cape is the strongest brand signal.
The stickers are the lowest-friction entry product.
The magnets may be the strongest parent-facing home display product.

The first test should compare demand across all three before choosing what to manufacture first.
