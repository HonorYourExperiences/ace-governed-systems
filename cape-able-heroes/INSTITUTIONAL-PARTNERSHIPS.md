# Cape-Able Heroes – Institutional Partnerships & Sponsorship (v1)

**Purpose:** Govern the approach to, outreach for, and tracking of large institutional partnerships and sponsorships — foundations, corporations, school districts, national youth organizations, and government programs.  
**Status:** v1 — framework active. No partnerships pursued yet.

---

## What This Is and Is Not

**This is:** A governed approach to high-leverage relationships that could fund, scale, or amplify Cape-Able Heroes at an institutional level — with full audit and alignment requirements.

**This is not:** A generic "get a sponsor" checklist. Institutional partnerships carry alignment risk. An organization whose brand or values conflict with the governing axioms is not a viable partner, regardless of funding amount.

The governing question for any institutional partnership: **Does this relationship extend the mission, or does it compromise it?**

---

## Types of Institutional Relationships

### 1. Program Sponsors

Organizations that provide financial support in exchange for recognition, co-branding, or access to Cape-Able Heroes programming for their constituents or employees.

**Examples:** Corporate CSR programs, foundations focused on children's development, community foundations, local businesses

**What they want:** Visible impact, brand alignment, community goodwill, employee or family engagement
**What we offer:** Co-branded WonderLab sessions, case studies with real outcomes, named recognition in materials

---

### 2. Curriculum or Program Partnerships

Organizations that integrate Cape-Able Heroes into their existing programming — not just funding it, but running it as part of their delivery model.

**Examples:** National youth organizations (Scouts, YMCA, Boys & Girls Clubs, 4-H), large after-school networks, church networks, municipal parks and recreation departments

**What they want:** Proven programming, SEL outcomes, ease of delivery, differentiation
**What we offer:** Institutional licensing (Tier 2 from LICENSING-PARTNER-PROGRAM.md), facilitator training, co-branded materials, impact documentation

---

### 3. Grant Funding

Foundations and government programs that provide grants for child development, SEL education, arts/culture programming, or community enrichment.

**Examples:** Local community foundations, state arts or education grants, national foundations focused on childhood or equity, SAMHSA-affiliated programs

**What they want:** Measurable outcomes, documented methodology, accountability
**What we offer:** Full audit trail from SESSION-LOG.md and TESTIMONIAL-CASE-STUDY.md, evidence base from Phase 1–3, documented methodology (SESSION-FORMAT.md + CHALLENGE-CARDS.md)

---

### 4. Media and Distribution Partners

Organizations that could amplify Cape-Able Heroes to large audiences — without compromising the integrity of the brand.

**Examples:** Parenting media, children's media brands, educational publishers, podcast networks focused on parenting or child development

**What they want:** Content, credibility, audience value
**What we offer:** Expert framing on identity and capability development, original content, story-based materials

## Alignment Filter (Required Before Any Partnership)

Before pursuing any institutional relationship, answer all five questions:

| Question | Standard |
|----------|----------|
| Does this organization's mission align with Inherent Sufficiency? | Children must not be treated as deficient or needing rescue |
| Does this relationship require messaging that conflicts with Way Through? | If yes, do not proceed |
| Will this partner require us to compromise Witnessing standards? | If the relationship requires performance over presence, do not proceed |
| Does this organization have public positions or practices that conflict with Evidence & Capability? | If they favor aspiration theater over real outcomes, proceed with caution |
| Can we maintain Audit Integrity in this relationship? | If the partner requires confidentiality about outcomes or restricts honest reporting, do not proceed |

**If any question produces a hard no, do not pursue the partnership — regardless of the financial value.**

Log the alignment review for every partnership pursued in the tracker below.

## Partnership Outreach Process

### Step 1 — Identify the Target
Log the organization in the tracker below. Describe what they want and what the alignment risk is.

### Step 2 — Alignment Review
Answer the five alignment questions above. Document the result in the tracker.

### Step 3 — Prepare a One-Page Overview
Every institutional outreach should be accompanied by a one-page overview of Cape-Able Heroes that includes:
- What it is (one paragraph — use the universal description from OUTREACH-TEMPLATES.md)
- Who it's for
- What outcomes it produces (use case study evidence from TESTIMONIAL-CASE-STUDY.md)
- What the partnership would look like
- Contact information

### Step 4 — Make Contact
Use warm introductions where available. Cold outreach works but is slower. Track every contact in the log below.

### Step 5 — Follow Up Systematically
One follow-up after 7 days of no response. One more after 14 days. After that, log as inactive and revisit in 90 days.

### Step 6 — Partnership Agreement
Any formal partnership (sponsorship or curriculum integration) requires a written agreement — even a simple one. The agreement must include:
- What Cape-Able Heroes will deliver
- What the partner will provide (funding, access, co-branding, etc.)
- Quality and governance standard acknowledgment
- Reporting and evidence commitments
- Term and renewal conditions

## What We Need Before Pursuing Institutional Partnerships

Institutional funders and large program partners require evidence. The following must be in place before serious institutional outreach begins:

- [ ] At least 3 paid activations documented in SESSION-LOG.md
- [ ] At least 1 full case study completed in TESTIMONIAL-CASE-STUDY.md
- [ ] A one-page program overview ready to share
- [ ] Documented methodology (already exists in SESSION-FORMAT.md + CHALLENGE-CARDS.md)
- [ ] Pricing and licensing structure defined (LICENSING-PARTNER-PROGRAM.md)

*When these are checked, institutional outreach can begin in earnest.*

---

## Governance Note

Any partnership that changes how Cape-Able Heroes operates — its session structure, its language, its identity-first model — requires a SAGA proposal and must be evaluated against the five core axioms before the agreement is signed. No exception.

Revenue from a partner who requires us to compromise the governing model is not revenue worth having.
