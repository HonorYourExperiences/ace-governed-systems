---

## Objective

Move Cape-Able Heroes from a founder-led, session-by-session business into a scalable, leveraged model — one that generates revenue and impact without requiring Rian to be physically present in every room.

---

## Current State (End of Phase 3 → Beginning of Phase 4)

- Paid WonderLab activations have been delivered across multiple buyer channels.
- Testimonials and at least one full case study document real outcomes.
- The highest-converting buyer channel has been identified from CHANNEL-TESTING-TRACKER data.
- The Educator/Facilitator Toolkit (Phase 2) exists and is ready for external deployment.
- Revenue is positive but tied directly to founder time and presence.
- No licensing or partner program exists.
- Digital components have not been built or formally evaluated.
- Sponsorship and large institutional partnerships have not been pursued.
- The product ecosystem (storybooks, digital tools) remains conceptual.

---

## Target State (End of Phase 4)

- A documented, active Licensing & Partner Program that allows other organizations or individuals to run Cape-Able Heroes programming under a governed standard.
- At least one digital component launched or formally evaluated and ruled in/out based on evidence.
- At least one sponsorship or major institutional partnership pursued and documented.
- A clear product ecosystem roadmap anchored in field evidence — not aspiration.
- Revenue no longer solely dependent on Rian's direct delivery presence.

## Gap-Closing Actions

### Action 1 — Launch Licensing & Partner Program
Design and deploy a governed licensing model so that trained facilitators, schools, camps, and organizations can run Cape-Able Heroes WonderLabs independently — under a defined quality and governance standard. Use LICENSING-PARTNER-PROGRAM.md as the operating document.

### Action 2 — Evaluate and Develop Digital Components
Do not build digital tools because it seems like a good idea. Evaluate them against a clear decision framework: Does a digital component extend the identity-as-architecture model in a way that cannot be done physically? If yes, build it. If no, do not build it yet. Use DIGITAL-STRATEGY.md to govern this evaluation.

### Action 3 — Pursue Sponsorship and Large Institutional Partnerships
Identify and approach organizations (foundations, corporations, school districts, national youth programs) that could fund, sponsor, or scale Cape-Able Heroes at an institutional level. Use INSTITUTIONAL-PARTNERSHIPS.md to govern the approach and track outcomes.

### Action 4 — Map the Product Ecosystem
Build a governed map of the long-term product vision — storybooks, digital tools, licensing tracks, curriculum packages — anchored to what has been proven in the field by Phase 3. Use PRODUCT-ECOSYSTEM.md as the living roadmap for this layer.
