# Proposal: Cape-Able Heroes Initial Development

**Date:** 2026-06-26
**Status:** Draft
**Type:** SAGA Gap-Closing Proposal

## Objective

Develop Cape-Able Heroes into a clear, repeatable, and sellable offer that helps children feel capable, recognized, and brave enough to step forward — while staying aligned with BYDT’s core axioms.

## Current State

- Cape-Able Heroes exists as a conceptual offering with strong philosophical grounding.
- Key ideas have been explored: identity as architecture, the Root Axiom, and the transformation of moving from hesitation to participation.
- A high-level business model and offer ladder have been drafted.
- A WonderLab session structure has been outlined (60–75 minutes, themeable challenges).
- Challenge cards and take-home materials have been conceptualized but not yet produced.
- No repeatable delivery format or pricing model has been tested in the market.
- Limited structured observation or field evidence has been captured.

## Desired State

- Cape-Able Heroes has a clear, documented core offer (starting with the WonderLab format).
- There is a defined first revenue-generating offer with pricing and packaging.
- Simple tools exist to capture field evidence during sessions.
- The offer is ready to be tested through 3–5 pilot or paid sessions.
- The positioning and language are clear enough to communicate to parents, schools, camps, and partners.
- The development work is governed and documented inside this repo.

## Key Gaps

- No finalized, tested WonderLab session format.
- No observation or measurement tools for early validation.
- No clear beachhead offer or pricing.
- Limited documentation of the full offer system (identity moment + challenge + reflection + artifact).
- No defined go-to-market approach for early pilots.

## Proposed First Actions

1. Finalize the Cape-Able Heroes WonderLab session format (60–75 minutes, themeable challenges).
2. Design and create simple printable Challenge Cards.
3. Develop basic observation tools to capture field evidence during pilots.
4. Run 3–5 pilot or paid WonderLab sessions.
5. Develop a take-home Hero Kit as a complementary product.
6. Begin light outreach to test interest from parents, schools, and camps.

## Alignment with Core Axioms

- **Inherent Sufficiency**: Cape-Able Heroes is built on the belief that children should not have to prove they are capable before being treated as capable.
- **Way Through**: The offer focuses on helping children take small, reachable actions and build self-trust through evidence rather than waiting to feel ready.
- **Witnessing & Mature Presence**: The model emphasizes recognition and creating space for children to be seen in their effort.
- **Evidence & Capability**: Strong emphasis on visible evidence of effort and participation.
- **Audit Integrity**: All development work will be documented and governed inside this repository.

## Success Metrics (First Phase)

- WonderLab format documented and ready to run.
- At least 3 pilot or paid sessions completed.
- Basic field evidence captured (participation, language use, observed shifts).
- Clear, communicable offer description created.
- Initial pricing and packaging tested.
