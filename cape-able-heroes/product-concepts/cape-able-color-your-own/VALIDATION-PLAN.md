# Cape-Able Color Your Own - Validation Plan

**Purpose:** Test whether the Color Your Own product family deserves to move from concept to build.

## Validation Question

Will real buyers pay for Cape-Able Color Your Own products as a continuation of the WonderLab identity experience?

## Test Order

### Test 1 - Post-Session Interest

**When:** After the next WonderLab session.

**Prompt to parent/host:**

> We are testing take-home Cape-Able products children can personalize after the session: a color-your-own cape, stickers, and magnets. Which one would you actually consider buying or adding to a future session?

**Measure:**

- Which variant they choose
- Whether they ask price
- Whether they would buy one child kit or group pack
- Exact language they use

### Test 2 - Soft Preorder

**Offer:** Simple preorder interest with no manufacturing promise.

**Minimum signal:**

- 3+ paid preorders, or
- 5+ written yes responses from parents/hosts

### Test 3 - Low-Cost Prototype

**Prototype options:**

- Printable cape template on paper before fabric production
- Sticker sheet printed locally
- Magnet sheet assembled from existing printable magnet paper

**Observe:**

- Does the child personalize it without heavy adult direction?
- Do they name what it means?
- Do they keep, wear, place, or show it afterward?
- Does the parent understand it as more than a craft?

## Decision Rules

| Signal | Decision |
|---|---|
| Parents like the idea but will not pay | Keep as session material only |
| Children use it but parents do not value it | Use as included artifact, not standalone product |
| Hosts ask for group packs | Prioritize classroom/party pack |
| Parents ask for individual kits | Prioritize direct-to-parent entry kit |
| Cape gets attention but stickers/magnets get purchase intent | Launch stickers/magnets first, cape as premium |

## Validation Metrics

| Metric | Target |
|---|---|
| Post-session parent interest | 5+ written yes responses |
| Paid preorder signal | 3+ paid preorders |
| Host/group pack signal | 1+ host asks for pack pricing |
| Child engagement | 70%+ complete personalization without rescue |
| Evidence quality | At least 3 exact parent/child quotes |

## What Not To Do Yet

- Do not place a large manufacturing order.
- Do not call this a proven product line.
- Do not claim confidence outcomes from the product alone.
- Do not treat a beautiful concept sheet as market proof.
