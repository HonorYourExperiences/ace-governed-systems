## What Is a WonderLab? (Universal One-Paragraph Description)

*Use this in any context — emails, conversations, social posts, booking pages.*

> Cape-Able Heroes WonderLab is a 60–75 minute guided experience where children build a hero identity, take on a real hands-on challenge, and leave with evidence — a Hero Card and badge that says: *I tried. I kept going. I have proof.* It's not a craft class or an assembly. It's a structured moment designed to move a child from hesitation to participation, using identity as the architecture. Parents leave knowing exactly what their child did — and children leave knowing they can try.

## Conversation Notes (What to Say When Someone Asks "What Is It Really?")

If they ask for the 30-second version:

> "It's a 60-minute experience where kids build a hero identity and do a real challenge. They don't earn the badge at the end — they get it at the start, because they showed up. Every child leaves with a Hero Card that says what they actually did. It's built for kids who hold back — and for parents who want to see their child step forward."

If they ask "Is it like a superhero party?":

> "Not quite. There are no costumes and no pretending to save the world. A Cape-Able Hero is just someone who tries something hard. The whole session is designed to help kids experience that — and walk away with proof that they did."

If they ask "Why would a school pay for this?":

> "Because SEL outcomes matter and visible evidence matters more. Kids leave with an artifact and a specific memory. Teachers and parents leave with a shared language to keep building on."
