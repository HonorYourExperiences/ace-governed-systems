## Fireworks / Cargo Aircraft Observation

One child created a paper airplane that carried paper confetti inside the fuselage. The child scribbled/color-marked paper, tore it into small pieces, placed the pieces inside the aircraft, and then flew the plane so the "cargo" released behind it during flight.

The aircraft was interpreted in the moment as an American-flag or fireworks-style aircraft because of the color and release effect.

---

## Why This Matters

This was not only decoration. The child invented a payload behavior:

- the aircraft had internal cargo
- the cargo affected the flight experience
- the release created a visible trail
- the design had a show/performance quality

The field signal is that children may naturally extend aircraft design beyond distance and accuracy. They may invent mission, cargo, visual identity, and flight effects when given enough material freedom.

---

## Method Implication

Future Aviation WonderLab sessions should leave room for:

- cargo/payload design
- air show effects
- mission-based aircraft design
- visual identity as part of engineering expression
- "what did your aircraft carry?" as a reflection question

This supports the Airline Founder and Aircraft Designer roles, and may justify a future role card:

**Payload Designer** — decides what the aircraft carries, releases, delivers, or represents.

## Guardrails

- Do not publicly identify the child without permission.

- Do not over-structure the activity so much that this kind of invention becomes impossible.
